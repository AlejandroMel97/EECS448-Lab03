Kobe Bryant

Kobe Bean Bryant (born August 23, 1978) is an American retired professional basketball player. He played his entire 20 year in the NBA with the Los Angeles lakers. He entered the league straight out of highschool. He was drafted in the 1996 draft and was chosen as the 13th overall pick but was then traded over to the Lakers. Bryant is an 18-time All-Star, 15-time member of the All-NBA Team, and 12-time member of the All-Defensive team. He led the NBA in scoring during two seasons, and ranks third on the league's all-time regular season scoring and fourth on the all-time postseason scoring list. He also holds league records such as scoring 81 points in a single game behind only Wilt ChamberLain with 100 points. Kobe Bryant also has a total of Points 33,643 averaging 25.0 ppg, 7,047 Rebounds averaging 5.2 rpg, and 6,306 assists averaging 4.7 apg. He has won a total of 5 championships as a Laker and recently had his two jerseys retired by the Laker Organization, which is the first time that a single player has ever gotten two jerseys retired for him.
